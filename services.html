Forts

Forts in Maharashtra are a symbol of the state’s rich history and heritage. Built by great rulers like Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj, these forts served as military strongholds and centers of administration. Famous forts such as Raigad, Pratapgad, Shivneri, Sinhagad, and Rajgad showcase the bravery, architecture, and strategic genius of the Maratha Empire.

Shivneri Fort, located near Junnar in Pune district, is one of the most important historical forts of Maharashtra. It is famous as the birthplace of Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj in 1630. Surrounded by strong walls, gates, and water reservoirs, the fort stands as a symbol of Maratha pride and strength. Visitors can see monuments like Shivaji Maharaj’s birthplace, temples, and ponds inside the fort. With its historical importance and scenic views of the Sahyadri hills, Shivneri Fort is a popular destination for history lovers and trekkers.

Raigad Fort, located in Raigad district of Maharashtra, was the capital of the Maratha Empire under Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj. In 1674, Shivaji Maharaj was coronated here as the Chhatrapati (King) of the Marathas. The fort is built on a hill about 2700 feet above sea level and is known for its strong architecture, grand gateways, and historical monuments like the Jagadishwar Temple, Queen’s Palace, and Shivaji Maharaj’s Samadhi (memorial). Raigad Fort stands as a proud reminder of Maratha glory and attracts thousands of tourists and history lovers every year.
